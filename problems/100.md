# Problem 100 — Poetry Compression: Romantic Nature

You are competing against other agents to design the best losslessly invertible encoding for 19th century English poetry.

**Category:** Romantic nature poetry — lyric poems celebrating the natural world, written by English Romantic poets including Wordsworth, Keats, Shelley, and Coleridge.

**Task:**
Design an encoding system — a language, scheme, or algorithm — that can:
1. Encode any poem from this category into a compressed representation
2. Decode that representation back to the exact original text (lossless round-trip)
3. Achieve the highest possible compression ratio on **poems you will never see**

**You are NOT given the test poems.** This is deliberate. You get only the category description above. Your `encode`/`decode` will be evaluated on a private, held-out set of complete poems from this category — chosen by the scorer, never shown to you. Reason about what poems of this kind look like: the diction, the recurring vocabulary, the metre and line structure, the archaic spellings and punctuation. A scheme that merely memorises specific texts will score zero on the held-out set; a scheme that genuinely models the category will generalise.

**What to build:**
Write a Python script to `/tmp/solution.py` that:
- Defines `encode(text: str) -> str`
- Defines `decode(encoded: str) -> str`
- Guarantees a lossless round-trip: `decode(encode(poem)) == poem` exactly, for any poem in this category (including ones you invent to self-test)

You may write your own sample poems to test your round-trip — but remember the real evaluation uses held-out poems, so don't overfit to your samples.

**Scoring:** `compression_ratio / (1 + total_tokens / 10000)`, where the scorer computes `compression_ratio = mean(len(original) / len(encoded))` over the held-out poems, `total_tokens` is the sum of agent and judge tokens across the /goal loop, and `compression_ratio` is **0 if the round-trip fails on any held-out poem**. More compression = better. Each 10,000 tokens halves your effective score.

**You are competing against other agents on this same problem.** Your score is absolute but ranking is relative. A quick correct solution that scores 1.5 beats a slow elaborate solution that scores 1.4.

**Rules:**
- No web search.
- Your `encode`/`decode` must run with no external dependencies beyond the Python stdlib.
- Do not go hunting the filesystem for a poem corpus — the held-out poems are not in your working directory. Build a general encoder from your own knowledge of the category.
- Write your script to `/tmp/solution.py`.
- When confident, stop and say DONE.
